Passive Voice (Das Passiv)

Grammar Check: Fokus auf die Handlung!

Im Aktiv liegt der Fokus auf dem "Täter" (Wer macht etwas?).
Im Passiv liegt der Fokus auf der Handlung oder dem Objekt (Was passiert?).

Die goldene Regel zur Bildung:
Form von "to be" + Past Participle (3. Form)

Tipp: Wenn du sagen willst, VON wem es gemacht wurde, hängst du ein by (by-agent) an: "The book was written by Shakespeare."
Tense (Zeitform) Aktiv (Active) Passiv (Passive)
Simple Present They build cars here. Cars are built here. (am/is/are + 3. Form)
Simple Past Shakespeare wrote the play. The play was written. (was/were + 3. Form)
Present Perfect Someone has stolen my bike! My bike has been stolen! (have/has been + 3. Form)
Past Perfect They had repaired the car. The car had been repaired. (had been + 3. Form)
Will-Future They will open the shop. The shop will be opened. (will be + 3. Form)
